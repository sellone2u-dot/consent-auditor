# Dealer Website Risk Auditor

## Vision

> **Truth over assumptions. Evidence over opinions. Education over fear.**

Dealer Website Risk Auditor is an educational website privacy assessment tool built to help organizations understand how their websites behave from a visitor's perspective.

The extension observes real browser behavior, reports only factual evidence, translates technical findings into plain English, and guides organizations toward stronger privacy compliance through education and recommended actions.

It is designed for dealership owners, managers, marketing teams, website providers, advertising agencies, consent platform providers, and privacy professionals.

---

# Mission

## Report the facts.

## Explain the facts.

## Recommend the next step.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

---

# Product Purpose

Dealer Website Risk Auditor exists to train organizations toward stronger privacy compliance.

It accomplishes this by:

- Observing real browser behavior
- Reporting only evidence supported by captured data
- Translating technical findings into plain English
- Providing practical recommendations for improvement

The goal is not to assign blame.

The goal is to improve understanding.

---

# Core Principles

## Report nothing but the facts.

Every statement in every report must be supported by captured browser evidence.

If the browser did not observe it, the report must not claim it.

---

## Be direct.

The report should communicate confidently when the evidence supports a conclusion.

Avoid unnecessary hedging when observable facts exist.

Example:

✅ Meta advertising request was observed before the visitor made a consent choice.

instead of

❌ Meta may have loaded before consent.

---

## Explain in plain English.

Every technical finding should be understandable by someone who has never opened Developer Tools.

The report should educate, not overwhelm.

---

## Preserve evidence.

Every observation should remain traceable to captured browser evidence, including:

- Network requests
- Cookies
- Browser storage
- Consent signals
- Browser APIs
- Captured metadata

Evidence should always remain available within the report.

---

## Educate.

Every finding should help users understand:

- What happened
- Why it happened
- Why it matters
- What should be reviewed next

---

## Recommend.

Recommend practical next steps rather than assigning blame.

Examples:

- Review with your website provider.
- Verify with your consent management vendor.
- Confirm your Google Consent Mode configuration.
- Review advertising technology load order.

---

# What this product is NOT

Dealer Website Risk Auditor is NOT:

- Legal advice
- A compliance certification
- A vulnerability scanner
- A cookie counter

It is an evidence-based browser assessment tool.

---

# Report Philosophy

Every finding should answer four questions.

## 1. What happened?

Report the observed browser evidence.

---

## 2. What does it mean?

Translate the technical finding into plain English.

---

## 3. Why does it matter?

Explain the finding within the current consent state.

---

## 4. What should happen next?

Provide a practical recommendation supported by the evidence.

# Product Standards

Every new feature should improve one or more of the following:

- Factual accuracy
- Evidence quality
- Clarity
- Education
- Usability
- Accessibility
- Performance

If a feature does not improve one of these areas, it likely does not belong in the product.

---

# Development Rules

Before implementing any feature, ask:

1. Can the browser observe it?
2. Can we prove it?
3. Can we explain it clearly?
4. Can we support every statement with evidence?
5. Can we recommend a useful next step?

If the answer to any of these questions is **No**, reconsider the implementation.

---

# The Rule

> **If the browser didn't observe it, don't report it.**

If the browser observed it, report it confidently.

Every report should be factual.

Every finding should be understandable.

Every recommendation should help organizations move toward stronger privacy compliance.

---

# Version 1.0 Goal

Deliver a browser extension that anyone can use to:

- Observe website privacy behavior
- Understand technical findings
- Communicate effectively with vendors
- Improve website privacy through evidence-based decisions

The success of this product will not be measured by the number of issues it finds.

It will be measured by how many organizations better understand their websites and improve their privacy practices because of it.
